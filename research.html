Research

Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of development economics, political economy, and area studies, with a focus on economies where reliable macroeconomic data are scarce or absent. The North Korean economy is the primary case — both for its intrinsic importance to Korean peninsula stability, and as a methodological laboratory for measuring economic activity under extreme information constraints.

A growing component of my research applies satellite-based remote sensing data (nighttime lights, daytime imagery, pollution indices, land surface temperature) to proxy economic variables in North Korea, where official statistics are unavailable or unreliable. I am also interested in the micro-level consequences of the North Korean system: how defectors navigate labor markets, financial systems, and healthcare in South Korea.

Research Areas

North Korean Market Dynamics

Price formation, exchange rate fluctuations, marketization indices, and the interaction between informal markets and state distribution systems. Data sources include refugee surveys and the Daily NK market price database.

Satellite-Based Economic Analysis

Using VIIRS/DMSP nighttime lights, Sentinel-2 daytime imagery, aerosol optical depth, and land surface temperature to estimate economic activity, industrial output, and food security conditions in North Korea.

Trade & Sanctions

North Korea–China trade dynamics, the effects of UN Security Council sanctions, firm-level analysis of South Korea–China trade, and the economic implications of North Korea–Russia cooperation.

North Korean Defectors

Labor market integration, financial behavior and credit access, mental and physical health outcomes, and national identity formation among North Korean refugees resettled in South Korea.

Food Security Assessment

Evolution of FAO/WFP assessment methodology (CFSAM → rFSA), NDVI and agricultural stress indices (ASI) as food production proxies, and indirect estimation approaches in the post-2019 period.

Inter-Korean Economic Integration

Public opinion on inter-Korean economic cooperation, geopolitical risk effects on financial markets, and historical and institutional perspectives on Korean economic integration.

Current Projects

Satellite-Based Economic Measurement of North Korea
Ongoing
Developing a composite economic activity index for North Korean provinces using multi-source satellite data (VIIRS nighttime lights, Sentinel-2 imagery, MODIS NDVI). The project evaluates the spatial and temporal distribution of economic recovery post-COVID and the impact of Russia–Ukraine War related military production.
North Korean Market Price Database
Ongoing
Maintaining and extending a structured database of North Korean market prices (rice, fuel, exchange rate) drawn from the Daily NK market monitoring network. The database covers 2010–present and is used for inflation estimation, dollarization analysis, and policy impact assessment.
Assessment of the 9th Korean Workers' Party Congress Economic Strategy
2026
Comparative analysis of economic directives from the 7th, 8th, and 9th Party Congresses, focusing on the shift from aggressive growth targets to stability-oriented "qualitative development" under conditions of domestic fiscal constraints and external geopolitical uncertainty.
