Research

Research Interests

My research focuses on the North Korean economy and its place in regional and global economic systems — an area where reliable macroeconomic data are scarce, official statistics are limited, and indirect measurement is essential. Across four interrelated themes, my work combines area-studies depth with empirical economic methods.

Research Areas

North Korean Economy

Marketization, price formation, the impact of border closures on domestic markets, and the evolution of economic strategy under successive Party Congresses.

Foreign Economic Relations: China & Russia

North Korea’s external economic ties, with particular focus on trade with China and the economic implications of strengthened North Korea–Russia relations amid the Russia–Ukraine war.

Unification & Economic Integration

The macroeconomic and financial-market consequences of inter-Korean geopolitical dynamics, plus historical and institutional perspectives on Korean economic integration.

North Korean Refugees

Labor-market assimilation, health status, and subjective well-being among North Korean defectors resettled in South Korea.

Current Projects

Assessment of the 9th Korean Workers’ Party Congress Economic Strategy
2026 · KDI North Korean Economic Review
Comparative analysis of economic directives from the 7th, 8th, and 9th Party Congresses, focusing on the shift from aggressive growth targets to stability-oriented “qualitative development” under conditions of domestic fiscal constraint and external geopolitical uncertainty.
Health and Economic Assimilation of North Korean Defectors
Forthcoming · Asian Perspective, Vol. 50, No. 3
Joint work with Jongmin Lee and HyeSeung Wee examining how physical and mental health status shape labor-market and economic outcomes for North Korean defectors after resettlement in South Korea.
Economic Implications of North Korea–Russia Cooperation
Ongoing
Extension of recent work (Korean Journal of Defense Analysis, 2024) on the economic dimensions of strengthened North Korea–Russia ties amid the Russia–Ukraine war, including trade flows, labor exports, and the broader sanctions-evasion landscape.
