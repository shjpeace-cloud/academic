Research Interests

My research focuses on the North Korean economy, with particular emphasis on market transition dynamics, price formation, and trade patterns under sanctions. I also work on satellite-based economic measurement — using nighttime lights, daytime imagery, and environmental data to proxy economic activity in information-scarce environments. A second strand of my work examines the economics of North Korean defectors: their labor market integration, financial behavior, and health outcomes in South Korea.

North Korean Economy

Market transition, price dynamics, sanctions effects, trade statistics, inter-Korean economic integration

Satellite-Based Measurement

Nighttime lights, daytime imagery, pollution and heat data as proxies for economic activity in DPRK

North Korean Defectors

Labor market participation, financial behavior, mental and physical health outcomes after resettlement

Geopolitics & Trade

Russia–Ukraine War implications, North Korea–Russia–China triangular relations, sanctions analysis
